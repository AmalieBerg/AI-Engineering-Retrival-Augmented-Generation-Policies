IT Acceptable Use Policy

Document ID: POL-IT-001   |   Version: 3.0   |   Effective: February 15, 2025

1. Purpose

This policy establishes acceptable use standards for Northwind Technologies' IT resources, including networks, systems, software, devices, accounts, and data. Adherence to this policy is a condition of access to Northwind systems.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all users of Northwind IT resources, including employees, contractors, interns, vendors with access, and any third parties granted system access.

3. General Acceptable Use

Northwind IT resources are provided primarily for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it:

  • Does not interfere with the employee's work or that of colleagues
  • Does not consume excessive bandwidth or storage
  • Does not violate any other Northwind policy
  • Does not expose Northwind to legal, reputational, or security risk

4. Prohibited Activities

The following uses of Northwind IT resources are strictly prohibited:

  1. Accessing, storing, or transmitting pornography, hate speech, or other illegal content
  2. Engaging in harassment, threats, or discrimination through any company system
  3. Installing or using software not approved by IT (see Section 7)
  4. Circumventing security controls, including VPN, firewall, or MDM
  5. Using Northwind resources for personal financial gain unrelated to employment
  6. Sharing account credentials with anyone, including other Northwind employees
  7. Sending mass unsolicited communications (spam) to external recipients
  8. Accessing systems or data for which the user has no business need
  9. Mining cryptocurrency on Northwind hardware or networks
  10. Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network

5. Email Use

Northwind email is the property of Northwind and is subject to monitoring and retention. Employees should not have an expectation of privacy in their company email. Use of email must:

  • Comply with the Information Security Policy (POL-SEC-001), particularly regarding data classification
  • Avoid forwarding business communications to personal accounts
  • Use BCC for recipient lists of more than 10 external addresses
  • Include an approved signature block for external communications

Auto-forwarding rules to external addresses are blocked at the email gateway. Requests for exceptions must be approved by the CISO.

6. Internet and Web Browsing

Internet access from Northwind networks is monitored. Northwind employs web filtering to block known malicious sites and categories that conflict with this policy. Categories blocked include: malware/phishing, adult content, illegal drugs, gambling, weapons sales, and unauthorized file-sharing services.

Employees may request access to a specific blocked site for business purposes by contacting helpdesk@northwind.example.

7. Software Installation

Only IT-approved software may be installed on Northwind devices. Employees may install software from the Northwind Self-Service Portal without additional approval. Software not in the portal requires a software request ticket and is subject to security and licensing review.

Use of unauthorized software (including free or open-source) is a violation of this policy. Particularly prohibited are:

  • VPN clients other than Cisco AnyConnect
  • Personal cloud storage clients (Dropbox personal, iCloud Drive, etc.) on company devices
  • Browser extensions not vetted by IT
  • Remote-access tools (TeamViewer, AnyDesk, etc.) other than the company-approved tools

8. AI and Generative Tools

Use of generative AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, etc.) is permitted only through Northwind's enterprise accounts. Employees must not paste Confidential or Restricted data into consumer AI tools. The approved enterprise tools are listed in the Self-Service Portal under "AI Tools."

Code generated by AI tools must be reviewed by the employee before use and is subject to the same code review and security standards as human-written code.

9. Social Media

Employees may use social media on personal time and devices, subject to the Social Media Guidelines (POL-COMMS-002). Employees must:

  • Not represent personal views as those of Northwind unless explicitly authorized
  • Not disclose Confidential or Restricted information
  • Not engage in harassment or discrimination, even on personal accounts, in a manner that could be associated with Northwind

10. Monitoring and Privacy

Northwind monitors network traffic, system access logs, email metadata, and endpoint activity for security and compliance purposes. Monitoring may be increased in response to suspected policy violations. By using Northwind systems, employees consent to this monitoring.

Northwind does not monitor:

  • The content of personal communications on personal devices outside the work container
  • Personal email or social media accounts
  • The content of webcam or microphone feeds unless during a recorded meeting

11. Violations

Violations of this policy may result in:

  • Suspension of system access
  • Disciplinary action up to and including termination
  • Civil or criminal action for serious violations

Suspected violations should be reported to helpdesk@northwind.example or, for security concerns, security@northwind.example.
