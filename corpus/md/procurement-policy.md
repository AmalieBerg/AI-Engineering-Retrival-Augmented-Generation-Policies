# Procurement Policy

**Document ID:** POL-FIN-002
**Version:** 3.3
**Effective Date:** May 1, 2025
**Owner:** Procurement & Finance

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the process by which Northwind Technologies procures goods and services from third parties. It is designed to ensure fiscal responsibility, vendor accountability, and compliance with applicable regulations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all purchases of goods or services on behalf of Northwind, including software subscriptions, professional services, hardware, office supplies, and capital equipment. It does not cover employee expense reimbursements, which are governed by POL-FIN-001.

## 3. Approval Thresholds

All purchases require approval based on total contract value (TCV):

| Total Contract Value | Required Approver |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Under $1,000 | Manager |
| $1,000 – $9,999 | Director |
| $10,000 – $49,999 | VP |
| $50,000 – $249,999 | C-suite executive |
| $250,000 – $999,999 | CFO + CEO |
| $1,000,000 and above | Board of Directors |

TCV includes the total value across the full contract term, including renewal options that are likely to be exercised.

## 4. Vendor Selection

For purchases exceeding **$25,000**, at least **three competitive bids** must be obtained. Exceptions apply to:

- Sole-source providers (with documented justification)
- Renewal of existing contracts where vendor performance is documented as satisfactory
- Emergency purchases approved by the CFO

The Procurement team manages the RFP process for purchases exceeding **$100,000**.

## 5. Software Procurement

All software purchases must be reviewed by:

- **IT:** for technical compatibility and security
- **Legal:** for contract terms
- **Information Security:** for compliance with POL-SEC-001

Free or trial software may be downloaded and tested only after IT approval. Use of unauthorized software is prohibited under POL-IT-001 (Acceptable Use Policy).

## 6. Vendor Onboarding

Before any payment can be issued to a new vendor, the vendor must complete:

1. The Northwind Vendor Onboarding Form
2. A W-9 (for U.S. vendors) or W-8 (for non-U.S. vendors)
3. A Security Review (if processing or accessing Northwind data)
4. A signed Master Services Agreement (MSA) or Statement of Work (SOW)

New vendor onboarding typically takes **10–15 business days**. Plan accordingly.

## 7. Purchase Orders

A Purchase Order (PO) is required for all purchases of **$5,000 or more**. POs must be issued through the Coupa procurement system and approved prior to vendor engagement. Vendors should not be authorized to begin work without an approved PO.

Invoices submitted by vendors without a referenced PO will be rejected.

## 8. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any personal or family relationship with a vendor or prospective vendor to Procurement and Legal. Employees may not participate in vendor selection decisions for vendors with whom they have a conflict.

## 9. Gifts from Vendors

Employees may not accept gifts from current or prospective vendors valued at more than **$100 per calendar year** in aggregate. Cash gifts, gift cards, or any payment that could be construed as a kickback are strictly prohibited. Modest meals provided during vendor meetings (up to $75 per person) are acceptable.

## 10. Contract Renewals

Auto-renewal clauses must be flagged at the time of contract review. The Procurement team will maintain a renewal calendar and notify the contract owner at least **90 days** before any auto-renewal date. Contract owners are responsible for evaluating renewal value before the renewal window closes.

## 11. Termination of Vendor Relationships

When ending a vendor relationship, the contract owner must:

- Provide notice per the contract terms
- Coordinate with IT to revoke vendor access
- Ensure data return or destruction per the DPA
- Confirm with Finance that all outstanding invoices are settled

## 12. Audit

Procurement records are subject to periodic audit by the Internal Audit team and external auditors. All POs, contracts, and vendor records must be retained for a minimum of **7 years**.
