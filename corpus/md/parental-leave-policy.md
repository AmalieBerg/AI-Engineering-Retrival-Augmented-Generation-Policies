# Parental Leave Policy

**Document ID:** POL-HR-003
**Version:** 2.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Owner:** People Operations

## 1. Purpose

Northwind Technologies recognizes that becoming a parent is one of the most significant transitions in an employee's life. This policy provides paid parental leave to support employees as they welcome a new child into their family.

## 2. Eligibility

To be eligible for paid parental leave, an employee must:

- Be a full-time or part-time employee of Northwind
- Have completed at least **12 months** of continuous service as of the leave start date
- Be the parent, adoptive parent, or legal guardian of the child

Both birthing and non-birthing parents are eligible. The policy applies equally to mothers, fathers, adoptive parents, and parents through surrogacy.

## 3. Leave Duration

### 3.1 Birthing Parents

Birthing parents are entitled to **20 weeks** of paid parental leave at 100% of base salary. This includes:

- **6–8 weeks** of medical recovery (short-term disability) following childbirth
- **12–14 weeks** of bonding leave

### 3.2 Non-Birthing Parents

Non-birthing parents (including adoptive parents and parents through surrogacy) are entitled to **16 weeks** of paid bonding leave at 100% of base salary.

### 3.3 Adoption and Foster Care

For adoptive parents, the 16-week leave begins on the date the child is placed in the home. Northwind also reimburses up to **$10,000** in adoption-related expenses per child, including agency fees, legal fees, and travel.

## 4. Taking Leave

Parental leave may be taken in one continuous block or split into up to **two non-consecutive blocks** within the first 12 months following the child's birth, adoption, or placement. Any split must be agreed upon with the employee's manager and People Operations at least 30 days in advance.

Leave must begin within 12 months of the qualifying event. Unused leave is forfeited after this period.

## 5. Notice Requirements

Employees should notify their manager and People Operations of their intent to take parental leave at least **8 weeks** before the anticipated start date when possible. For unexpected events (premature birth, emergency placement), notice should be given as soon as reasonably possible.

A leave plan, including coverage arrangements and handoff documentation, should be completed at least 2 weeks before the leave begins.

## 6. Benefits Continuation

During parental leave, employees continue to receive:

- Full medical, dental, and vision insurance coverage
- Continued PTO accrual
- Continued vesting of equity grants
- 401(k) contributions on paid leave amounts
- Eligibility for any compensation adjustments or bonuses occurring during the leave period

## 7. Return-to-Work Support

Northwind offers a **gradual return-to-work program** under which an employee may work a reduced schedule (60% to 80% of full-time hours, at proportional pay) for up to **8 weeks** after returning from parental leave. Employees on a gradual return remain eligible for full benefits.

Lactation rooms are available in all Northwind offices. Remote employees may expense up to **$500** for a portable breast pump or related equipment.

## 8. Pregnancy-Related Accommodations

Employees experiencing pregnancy-related medical conditions may request reasonable accommodations under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and the Pregnant Workers Fairness Act (PWFA). Accommodations may include schedule adjustments, modified duties, or additional break time.

## 9. Job Protection

Employees returning from parental leave will be reinstated to the same position they held prior to leave, or to a comparable position with equivalent pay, benefits, and seniority. This protection extends through the full duration of the leave plus any approved gradual return period.

## 10. Interaction with Other Leave

Parental leave runs concurrently with FMLA (in the U.S.) and equivalent statutory entitlements in other jurisdictions. Where statutory leave exceeds the Northwind benefit, the longer entitlement applies. Northwind makes up any pay gap between statutory benefits and the employee's full base salary.
