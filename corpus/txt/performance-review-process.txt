PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS
==========================

Document ID: POL-HR-005
Version: 2.2
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Owner: People Operations


1. PURPOSE
----------
Northwind Technologies uses a structured performance review process to provide regular feedback, support employee development, and inform compensation and promotion decisions. This document describes the process, timing, and ratings.


2. REVIEW CYCLE
---------------
Northwind operates on a biannual review cycle:

  - H1 Review:  Conducted in July (covers performance from January-June)
  - H2 Review:  Conducted in January (covers performance from July-December)

In addition, all employees receive a quarterly informal check-in with their manager focused on goals, blockers, and development priorities.


3. RATING SCALE
---------------
Performance is rated on a five-point scale:

  5 - Significantly Exceeds Expectations
      Consistently delivers exceptional outcomes that materially impact the
      business. Represents the top 5-10% of employees at their level.

  4 - Exceeds Expectations
      Consistently goes beyond what is required of the role. Strong candidate
      for promotion within 6-12 months.

  3 - Meets Expectations
      Fully delivers on the responsibilities of the role. The majority of
      employees fall in this category.

  2 - Partially Meets Expectations
      Delivers on some but not all responsibilities. Requires a development
      plan with specific improvement targets.

  1 - Does Not Meet Expectations
      Substantial gaps in performance. Will be placed on a Performance
      Improvement Plan (PIP).


4. REVIEW COMPONENTS
--------------------
Each formal review includes:

  a) Self-Assessment
     The employee completes a self-assessment in Lattice covering
     achievements, areas for growth, and development goals. Due 2 weeks
     before the review meeting.

  b) Manager Assessment
     The manager evaluates the employee against role-specific expectations
     and assigns a rating. Due 1 week before the review meeting.

  c) Peer Feedback
     Each employee selects 3-5 peers to provide structured feedback.
     Manager may add additional peer reviewers. Peer feedback is shared
     in summarized form, not verbatim.

  d) Upward Feedback (managers only)
     Direct reports of managers provide upward feedback about the
     manager's leadership effectiveness.

  e) Calibration
     Before ratings are finalized, managers participate in calibration
     sessions to ensure consistency across teams.

  f) Review Meeting
     A 60-minute meeting between employee and manager to discuss the
     review and align on next-cycle goals.


5. COMPENSATION DECISIONS
-------------------------
Compensation reviews occur once per year, in March, following the H2 review:

  - Merit increases:    Based on H1 + H2 ratings, market data, and budget
  - Equity refreshes:   Targeted at 4-rated and 5-rated employees, plus
                        retention-critical roles
  - Bonuses:            Determined by individual rating and company performance

Mid-year (July) is reserved for promotions and equity adjustments tied to
role changes.


6. PROMOTION PROCESS
--------------------
Promotions require:

  - Two consecutive review cycles at the "Exceeds" level or higher, OR
    one cycle at "Significantly Exceeds"
  - Demonstration of scope at the next level for at least 6 months
  - Manager nomination via the promotion packet template
  - Approval by the promotion committee for the relevant level

Promotion decisions are communicated within 30 days of the committee meeting.


7. PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)
-------------------------------------
Employees rated 1 or who fail to meet critical job requirements may be
placed on a PIP. A PIP includes:

  - Specific, measurable improvement targets
  - A defined timeline of 30, 60, or 90 days
  - Weekly check-ins with the manager
  - Clear consequences if targets are not met (which may include termination)

PIPs are designed to be fair and to give the employee a genuine opportunity
to improve. Employees on a PIP may request People Operations involvement at
any time.


8. APPEALS
----------
Employees who believe their review was conducted unfairly may submit an
appeal to People Operations within 14 days of the review meeting. The appeal
will be reviewed by the People Operations Director and the employee's
skip-level manager. Appeal decisions are typically issued within 21 days.


9. CONFIDENTIALITY
------------------
Review content is confidential and is shared only with:

  - The employee and their manager
  - The skip-level manager
  - People Operations
  - The promotion or calibration committee (in aggregate form)

Employees may share their own review content as they wish.


10. RECORD RETENTION
--------------------
Performance review records are retained for 7 years following the review
cycle, or for the duration of employment plus 7 years, whichever is longer.

For questions about the review process, contact people-ops@northwind.example.
